<p style="text-align:center;">
Morality requires subtlety<br>
Righteous conduct needs awareness<br>
from inside oneself<br>
<br>
Outside rules can help with guidance<br>
Fear of outside punishment<br>
might provide incentive<br>
<br>
Both, though,<br>
have most people thinking<br>
of how they could escape from them<br>
<br>
Simple rules<br>
could give people guidance<br>
of how to respect themselves and others<br>
Rare punishment could provide incentive<br>
of finding a constructive way of conduct<br>
<br>
Morality, though<br>
was built inside of us by evolution<br>
as soon as human societies did form<br>
<br>
A system of reward and punishment<br>
for social conduct and relations<br>
is already wired into our brain<br>
<br>
The system though needs training<br>
<br>
Temptation may cause confusion<br>
When lesser things are more rewarded<br>
When higher things are punished by neglect<br>
<br>
The system will respond to harmony<br>
<br>
To guidance by example<br>
To actions that make sense<br>
<br>
Harmony<br>
Leading to feeling good for actions<br>
which bring rewards for ourselves and all<br>
<br>
Harmony<br>
Causing little nudges of feeling bad<br>
when we don’t behave so well
</p>
