<p>
Twenty-eight years ago,<br>
it was you<br>
&emsp;who first arrived to the United States<br>
&emsp;&emsp;from the island of Tobago.<br>
Despite the congestion, the frenzied pace and neon glare,<br>
it was you<br>
&emsp;who continued to grow deeper and more friendly.<br>
<br>
It was you<br>
&emsp;who was a carpenter with a thirst for God's songs.<br>
<br>
It was you<br>
&emsp;whose loving heart was solid<br>
&emsp;&emsp;like the foundation of an elementary school building.<br>
<br>
It was you<br>
&emsp;who was a man able to tickle<br>
&emsp;&emsp;the grey skies of daily life<br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;then gaze, like a curious child,<br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;at the pouring laughter.<br>
<br>
It was you<br>
&emsp;who was also an inconspicuous poet,<br>
&emsp;&emsp;extracting the heart of each word<br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;to arouse pure joy<br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;immersed in sensitivity to others.<br>
<br>
It was you<br>
&emsp;a simple yet complex man<br>
&emsp;&emsp;who gave without a thought.<br>
<br>
It was you<br>
&emsp;who was as graceful and, yes, as noble<br>
&emsp;&emsp;as you were big and strong.<br>
<br>
It was you<br>
&emsp;who was able to live<br>
&emsp;&emsp;free of frustration,<br>
&emsp;so relaxed and satisfied in your being<br>
&emsp;&emsp;and just in being.<br>
<br>
It was you indeed<br>
&emsp;who was the "professor"<br>
&emsp;&emsp;teaching by way of his own example.<br>
<br>
And we who had a glimpse of you are forever grateful.
</p>
