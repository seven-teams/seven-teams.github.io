<p>
Oh, ancient people, oh, ancient people, chosen by God,<br>
Leave all your fears even when fate has brought tears.<br>
Blessed by wit and blessed by wisdom - take your chance,<br>
And for your cause, on the shafts the of rose, you do your dance.<br>
<br>
Oh, ancient people, your sad laughter can burn the sea,<br>
So, tune your soul to make the play joyful for all to see.<br>
Marry in this drama a bee with the tiger, and eagle with a fish;<br>
Let the rains cry and let the winds dance at your heart’s wish.<br>
<br> 
Refrain:
So live and hope and laugh much more often at your fate!<br>
By wind of hurricane forgive all wrongs done, and then celebrate!<br>
Laugh at your enemies and laugh at yourself louder than all!<br>
Blessed by love and cleansed by the blood of this holy laughter.<br>
<br> 
Oh, ancient people, as priests leaf though scriptures,<br>
&emsp; you leaf though this age.<br>
Now read the sky where stars are your wounds, and count page after page.<br>
You were scorched by a wind, and burnt by the sun, and tamed by blame,<br>
But the villains are mortal - the crooks come to perish, but the people<br>
remain!<br>
<br> 
<b>Refrain:</b><br>
So live and hope and laugh much more often at your fate!<br>

<br>

</p>
